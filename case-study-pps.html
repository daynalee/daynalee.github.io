Overview

As Healthcare Operations Manager at a pain management clinic during COVID, I owned patient care coordination and the end-to-end intake experience. With rising patient volume, staffing constraints, and new safety protocols, the existing intake process had become a bottleneck. I led a redesign of clinical and administrative workflows to get patients seen faster without compromising care quality or compliance.

Context

Problem Statement. Patient intake was slow and inconsistent — paperwork, insurance verification, and scheduling were handled in disconnected steps that created delays, frustrated patients, and added administrative load on clinical staff during an already high-pressure period.

My Role. I was responsible for managing patient care coordination, redesigning intake workflows, and ensuring accurate, compliant insurance claims submission — balancing operational efficiency with the human side of patient care.

Project Goals.

  • Primary: Reduce patient intake time without compromising care quality or safety
  • Secondary: Reduce administrative bottlenecks and manual rework for clinical staff
  • Maintain accurate, timely insurance claims submission
  • Improve the patient experience from first contact to first appointment

Research

Workflow Audit. Mapped the full intake journey — from initial inquiry through paperwork, insurance verification, and scheduling — to identify where time was lost and where steps were duplicated.

Bottleneck Analysis. Identified the highest-friction points: incomplete intake forms, manual insurance verification, and back-and-forth scheduling that delayed first appointments.

Stakeholder Feedback. Gathered input from front desk staff, clinicians, and billing to understand pain points on both the operational and patient-facing sides.

Metrics for Success.

  • Average patient intake time
  • Reduction in administrative rework
  • Accuracy and timeliness of claims submission
  • Patient satisfaction with the intake experience

Challenges

Operating During COVID.

  • New safety protocols added steps and limited in-person processing capacity
  • Staffing constraints meant fewer people handling rising patient volume

Process & Data Gaps.

  • Intake paperwork, insurance verification, and scheduling were handled in disconnected steps
  • Incomplete or inaccurate intake information caused downstream delays and claims rework

Patient Experience.

  • Long intake times frustrated patients already managing chronic pain
  • Repeated requests for the same information eroded trust and satisfaction

Approach

Workflow Redesign. Restructured the intake process so paperwork, insurance verification, and scheduling moved in parallel rather than sequentially — front-loading information collection so patients arrived ready to be seen.

Reducing Administrative Bottlenecks. Streamlined hand-offs between front desk, clinical, and billing teams, eliminating duplicate data entry and clarifying ownership at each step.

Claims Accuracy. Tightened the link between intake data and billing so insurance claims were submitted accurately and on time, reducing rejections and rework.

Change Management. Trained and supported staff through the new workflow, keeping care quality and patient trust front and center throughout the transition.

Results

  • Reduced patient intake time by 25% through workflow redesign
  • Cut administrative bottlenecks and duplicate work for clinical and front-desk staff
  • Submitted $3M+ in annual insurance claims with high accuracy and compliance
  • Maintained quality of care and patient trust under high-pressure COVID conditions
